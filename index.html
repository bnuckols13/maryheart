THE 33-DAY MARIAN-BODHISATTVA
CONSECRATION
Quick Start Guide: Method, Prayers & Structure

Main Point 1: What Is This Practice?

A 33-day preparation culminating in total consecration to Mary as Bodhisattva
Synthesizes Marian devotion with Buddhist Bodhisattva practice through liberation theology
Prepares you to live as instrument of compassion working for universal liberation
Core Commitment: "I am all yours, Mary, and all that I have is yours—my body, my goods, my merits, my actions. Use me as your instrument for the liberation of all beings."
Mary as Bodhisattva: One who achieved complete awakening (theosis) but remains accessible to suffering beings
She appears at Lourdes, Guadalupe, Fatima—manifesting where beings need her
The Magnificat announces social revolution: "He has brought down the powerful from their thrones"
Parallel Frameworks: Christian kenosis (self-emptying) meets Buddhist emptiness practice
Mary's fiat ("let it be done to me") = taking the Bodhisattva vow
Christ consciousness = Buddha-nature: the awakened awareness already present in you

Main Point 2: The 33-Day Structure

The Four Periods

1
Renunciation
Days 1-12
Release worldly attachments: reputation, possessions, comfort, control
2
Self-Knowledge
Days 13-19
Honest recognition of afflictions: anger, craving, ignorance, shame
3
Knowledge of Mary
Days 20-26
Understanding Mary as Bodhisattva, intercessor, liberation worker
4
Knowledge of Christ
Days 27-33
Recognizing Christ consciousness/Buddha-nature as your actual condition
Day 34: The Consecration Ceremony (ideally on a Marian feast day or personally significant date)
Day 35+: Daily renewal practice for the rest of your life

Main Point 3: Daily Practice Structure (20-30 minutes)

Morning: Core prayers (10 minutes)
Throughout the day: Specific practice focus from daily reading (5-10 minutes)
Meditation: Your regular sitting practice (10-20 minutes)
Evening: Dedication of merit (5 minutes)
⚠ Integration Note: This supplements your existing meditation practice—it doesn't replace it. The morning consecration can precede your regular sitting. The evening dedication follows it.

Main Point 4: Core Daily Prayers (All 33 Days)

Prayer 1: Morning Consecration (Short Form)

Mother Mary, Bodhisattva of infinite compassion,
I renew my commitment to you this day.
I am yours completely—body, soul, merits, and all actions.
Use me as your instrument for the liberation of all beings.

Through you may I carry bodhicitta—the awakened heart—
Into every interaction, every choice, every moment.

May whatever I accomplish today benefit all beings.
May I face suffering with your courage,
Serve others with your compassion,
And never abandon the path until all beings are free.

Tuus totus ego sum, et omnia mea tua sunt.
I am all yours, and all that I have is yours.

Prayer 2: Bodhicitta Aspiration (3x daily)

Just as all the buddhas of the past
Aroused the awakened heart of bodhichitta
And progressively established themselves
In the training of a bodhisattva,

So, for the benefit of all beings,
I will arouse the awakened heart
And progressively train myself
In those disciplines.

Prayer 3: The Magnificat

My soul magnifies the Lord,
And my spirit rejoices in God my Savior.

For he has regarded the lowliness of his handmaiden;
For behold, henceforth all generations shall call me blessed.

For he that is mighty has done great things to me;
And holy is his name.

And his mercy is on those who fear him
From generation to generation.

He has shown strength with his arm;
He has scattered the proud in the imagination of their hearts.

He has put down the mighty from their thrones,
And exalted those of low degree.

He has filled the hungry with good things;
And the rich he has sent away empty.

He has helped his servant Israel, in remembrance of his mercy;
As he spoke to our fathers, to Abraham and to his seed forever.
Recognition: The Magnificat is Mary's prophecy of social liberation—the revolutionary reversal that the Kingdom of Heaven/Shambhala will accomplish. This is political theology, not just private devotion.

Prayer 4: Evening Dedication

Whatever virtue I have accomplished today—
Through generosity, ethics, patience, effort, meditation, or wisdom—
I dedicate completely to the liberation of all beings.

Through Mary's intercession, may this small offering
Become cause for countless beings' awakening.

May all beings have happiness and the causes of happiness.
May all beings be free from suffering and the causes of suffering.
May all beings never be separated from joy beyond suffering.
May all beings rest in equanimity, free from attachment and aversion.

Main Point 5: Additional Period-Specific Practices

Days 13-33: Add 5 decades of the Rosary daily (or 540 repetitions of Om Mani Padme Hum)
If you don't have a rosary, use a mala or count on fingers
This builds contemplative capacity and devotional connection
Days 20-26: Add 10 minutes of Marian contemplation
Sit with an image of Mary or simply hold her presence in awareness
Practice devotion not as worship of external deity but as opening to the configuration she represents

Main Point 7: How to Begin

Step 1: Choose Your Dates
Count backward 34 days from your chosen consecration date
Consider Marian feast days: Assumption (Aug 15), Immaculate Conception (Dec 8), Our Lady of Guadalupe (Dec 12)
Or choose a personally meaningful date (birthday, sobriety anniversary, etc.)
Step 2: Set Up Your Practice Space
Designate meditation area if you don't already have one
Keep guide, journal, and rosary/mala in one place
Optional: Add image of Mary, candle, or other meaningful objects
Step 3: Commit to the Structure
20-30 minutes daily minimum—schedule it like any other commitment
Morning prayers best done before other activities
Evening dedication before bed or after meditation practice
Step 4: Read Day 1
Each day includes: reading, commentary, practice instructions, meditation guidance, journal prompt
Don't skip ahead—the progression is intentional
If you miss a day, just continue where you left off (don't restart)
Sample Day 1 Schedule:

Morning (15 minutes):
• Read Day 1: "The Two Kingdoms" (5 min)
• Recite core prayers 1-3 (10 min)

Throughout Day:
• Notice choices based on accumulation vs. liberation
• Practice awareness of "spirit of the world" operating

Evening (15 minutes):
• Meditation: 20 min compassion practice holding Mary's quality
• Evening dedication prayer (5 min)
• Journal prompt: Three instances of worldly spirit today

Main Point 8: Common Questions

Q: Do I need to be Catholic or Buddhist to do this?

No. This practice works across traditions or outside formal religious identity
If Catholic: This deepens traditional Marian devotion through Buddhist wisdom
If Buddhist: This provides accessible theistic framework for compassion practice
If neither: This offers a complete contemplative path combining both lineages

Q: What if I miss a day?

Just continue—don't restart from Day 1
The path accommodates human limitation; perfectionism is an obstacle, not a virtue
If you miss several days, consider whether this is the right time to begin

Q: I already have a meditation practice. How does this fit?

This supplements rather than replaces your current practice
Morning consecration precedes your sitting (5-10 min)
Evening dedication follows your sitting (5 min)
Daily readings and practice focus inform but don't substitute for your core practice

Q: What happens after Day 34?

You renew the consecration daily using the short morning form
Continue evening dedications
The 33 days are preparation; Day 34+ is the actual practice of living the vow
Renew formally each year on your consecration anniversary

Main Point 9: What This Accomplishes

Not: Achieving perfect spiritual states or becoming instantly enlightened
Not: Earning Mary's favor or God's approval through performance
Not: Escaping ordinary life into transcendent experience
The Actual Goal: Becoming an instrument of liberation—someone who works for all beings' freedom from suffering, who faces reality without defensive retreat, who gives everything away rather than grasping for private salvation.
You'll still have afflictions—anger, craving, ignorance won't disappear
You'll still fail constantly—ego-grasping reasserts itself daily
What changes is directionality: You're oriented toward liberation rather than accumulation
Through Mary, you have support for returning to this orientation when you wander
"I am all yours, Mary, and all that I have is yours—not because I've perfected myself, but because you accept me as I actually am and transform me into an instrument of your compassion."
